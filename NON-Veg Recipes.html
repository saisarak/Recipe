Chicken Biryani Recipe


  1. Heat the oil in a large saucepan over a low heat. Add the onion, cover and cook for 10 minutes until softened.
  2. Cut the chicken into bite-size pieces, tip into a bowl, and add the lemon juice, garam masala, chilli powder and salt. Stir well to coat.
  3. Rinse the rice several times in cold water, until the water runs clear. Add the garlic and ginger to the onion and cook for 2 minutes, then add the chicken pieces and cook for a further 2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  4. Add the rice and hot stock to the pan, stir well then bring to the boil. Cover with a tight-fitting lid, reduce the heat slightly and cook for 5 minutes. Turn off the heat and leave the pan for 10 minutes to allow the rice to finish cooking in the steam. Do not remove the lid. Stir through the coriander and serve immediately.





Chicken Lollipop


    Marination:

  1. In a bowl, marinate the chicken lollipops with black pepper, ginger-garlic paste, salt, vinegar, soy sauce, and red chilli paste.
  2. Coat the chicken thoroughly with the marinade and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. After marination, add corn flour, refined flour, and egg to the chicken. Mix well to ensure the marinade sticks properly to the chicken.

    4. Frying:

  4. Heat oil in a pan for deep frying.
  5. Once the oil is hot, fry the chicken lollipops until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Optionally, you can coat the chicken with a light layer of dry flour before frying to make it extra crispy.
  7. Once fried, set the chicken aside.

    8. Sauce:

  8. In a separate pan, heat some oil.
  9. Add dry red chillies and chopped spring onion bulbs. Saute until aromatic.
  10. Add chopped garlic and sauté until golden brown.
  11. Then add finely chopped onions and cook until light brown.
  12. Next, add red chilli paste, red chilli sauce, ketchup, and a little water. Cook until well combined.
  13. Season with salt, pepper, sugar, vinegar, and soy sauce. Bring it to a boil.
  14. Add chopped spring onion bulbs, ginger julienne, and freshly chopped coriander leaves to the sauce. Mix well.
  15. Toss the fried chicken lollipops into the sauce.
  16. Dilute corn flour in water and add it to the mixture to thicken the sauce. Mix well and turn off the heat.

    17. Serving:

  17. Garnish the chicken lollipops with spring onion greens.
  18. Serve hot and enjoy your crispy fried chicken lollipops with the delicious sauce!
    19. When ready to cook, preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate. Remove chicken from marinade. Discard remaining marinade.





Chicken Tandoori


  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Remove and discard skin from chicken pieces. Cut slits into meat and place into a shallow dish. Season chicken on both sides with lemon juice and salt. Let sit for 20 minutes.
  3. Mix yogurt, onion, garlic, garam masala, ginger, and cayenne pepper together in a medium bowl until smooth, then stir in food coloring.
  4. Spread yogurt mixture over chicken, cover, and refrigerate for 6 to 24 hours
  5. When ready to cook, preheat an outdoor grill for medium-high heat and lightly oil the grate. Remove chicken from marinade. Discard remaining marinade.
  6. Cook chicken on the preheated grill until no longer pink and the juices run clear. An instant-read thermometer inserted near the bone should read 165 degrees F
  7. Garnish with cilantro and lemon wedges.





Fish Curry


  1. To make a curry paste, blend together the 3 red finger chillies, 1/2 of the ground coriander, the cumin, turmeric, garlic, ginger and tamarind paste until smooth, using either a food processor or pestle and mortar.
  2. Cut the pollock fillets into halves (or chunky pieces if really large), then season well with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat the oil to medium/high in a deep frying pan, add the onion and fry for about 5 minutes, until golden brown. Then add the split chillies, curry paste, the other 1/2 of the ground coriander and a little more oil if necessary, slowly stirring everything together.
  4. Mix in the tomato and fry for 1 minute, then add the coconut milk and season with more salt and pepper before reducing the heat.
  5. Add the fish to the pan, cover and simmer gently over a medium heat for about 5-6 minutes, turning the fish over half-way through. Add the fresh coriander and season again with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve in bowls with boiled rice and naan bread as sides.
